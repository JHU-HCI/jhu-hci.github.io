A recent New York Times article boldly stated that the Golden Age of Design is upon us. This talk will reflect a walk through the discipline of design's many eras and shifts — from user-centered design to experience design, service design, and systems design — to understand the migration from designing products to designing platforms. An alternative framing, product-service ecologies, will be introduced to stress a systemic and ecological view as a design approach to the products, services, environments, and platforms of today.

Jodi Forlizzi is the Geschke Director and a Professor of Human-Computer Interaction in the School of Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University. She is responsible for establishing design research as a legitimate form of research in HCI. Jodi is a member of the ACM CHI Academy and has been honored by the Walter Reed Army Medical Center for excellence in HRI design research. She has consulted with Disney and General Motors to create innovative product-service systems.
