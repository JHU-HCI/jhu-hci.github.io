Humans interact with other humans or the world through information from various channels including vision, audio, language, haptics, etc. To simulate intelligence, machines require similar abilities to process and combine information from different channels to acquire better situation awareness, better communication ability, and better decision-making ability. In this talk, we describe three projects covering multimodal user understanding, visual dialog optimization, and social multimodal dialog generation.

Zhou Yu is an Assistant Professor at the Computer Science Department at UC Davis. She received her PhD from Carnegie Mellon University in 2017. Zhou is interested in building robust and multi-purpose dialog systems. Her work on persuasive dialog systems received an ACL 2019 best paper nomination. She was featured in Forbes as 2018 30 Under 30 in Science, and her team won the 2018 Amazon Alexa Prize for a $500,000 cash award.
